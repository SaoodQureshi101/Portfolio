Campus Wayfinding Redesign

Role: UX researcher and interaction designer

Timeline: 5 weeks

Goal: Help first-time visitors find key campus buildings without staff assistance.

Problem

Visitors reported high stress when arriving for appointments because maps, signs, and web labels used different names for the same locations. They often asked staff for directions after multiple failed attempts.

Process

  • Interviewed 8 first-time visitors and 3 front-desk staff members
  • Mapped common route failures between parking, entrances, and service desks
  • Created a naming standard and visual hierarchy for signs and digital map labels
  • Validated revised flow with paper prototypes and timed walkthroughs

Outcome

In moderated tests, first-try route completion increased from 42% to 81%. Average time to destination dropped from 11 minutes to 6 minutes. Staff interruptions for direction questions decreased during peak hours.

What I would improve next

Add multilingual sign variants and run an accessibility audit focused on low-vision contrast at varying daylight conditions.
